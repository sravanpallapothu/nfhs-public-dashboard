General tips
  1. Pick a domain (e.g. Maternal health) to narrow down the indicator list.
  2. Select an indicator — a green or red pill will tell you if higher or lower values are better.
  3. Choose a state and a survey round, then click Show snapshot.
  4. You'll see 8 cards: the national value, your state's value, and the highest and lowest performing states and districts — both nationally and within your selected state.
  5. Where data exists for a previous round, each card shows a change arrow (e.g. ↑ 4.2pp) coloured green for improvement, red for deterioration.
  1. Pick a domain and indicator.
  2. Choose a geography level: National, State, or District.
  3. If you select State or District, additional dropdowns will appear — choose your state and (optionally) district.
  4. Click Show trend to see how the indicator has changed across NFHS rounds.
  5. The blue line is your selected geography. The red line is always India. At district level, the violet line is the parent state.
  6. Use the Download buttons to save the chart as a PNG or the data as a CSV.
  1. Pick a domain and indicator.
  2. Choose Country → states to compare all states, or State → districts to compare districts within one state.
  3. Select a survey round and a sort order (highest or lowest first).
  4. Click Show chart to see a ranked horizontal bar chart with value labels on each bar.
  5. A violet bar marks the reference benchmark — India's national value (Country → states) or the state average (State → districts) — so you can immediately see how many geographies fall above or below it.
  6. Use the Download buttons to save the chart or the data. The reference bar is excluded from the CSV.
  1. Pick a domain, indicator, and survey round.
  2. Choose a map type:
    • India — states: all states coloured by their value.
    • India — all districts: every district nationally (labels suppressed to reduce clutter; hover for name and value).
    • State — districts: districts within one state, with name and value labels where space allows.
  3. Click Show map. The header card inside the map shows the indicator, direction, round, mean, SD, and the reference value (national average for state/district maps; state average for state→district maps).
  4. Colours are based on standard deviation from the mean:
    • Green — best performers (more than 1 SD better than average).
    • Amber — within 1 SD of the mean.
    • Red — worst performers (more than 1 SD worse than average).
    • Grey — no data available.
  5. Hover over any region to see its name and exact value in a tooltip.
  6. Zoom and pan using your mouse scroll wheel and drag.
  7. Use ⬇ PNG to download the map including the header card and legend. Use ⬇ CSV to download the underlying data (includes Parent State column for district maps).
  1. Green arrow ↑ = improvement, red arrow ↓ = deterioration — the direction is indicator-aware, so a falling stunting rate is shown in green even though the number went down.
  2. Cards or values showing No data mean that particular geography was not covered in that round — this is common for districts in earlier rounds (NFHS-1/2/3).
  3. Some newer districts (carved out after 2016) may not appear on the choropleth map as separate units if NFHS-5 did not publish a separate factsheet for them.
  4. All data comes from the National Family Health Survey (NFHS), conducted by the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India.
  5. For questions or to report an error, contact the dashboard team.
